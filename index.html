How does WFP compare to the Wild West?

The Players

The Scouts

Forecasters watch road ahead. They look for what's around the next bend, what's over the next ridge. What happened last time we were here, Will it happen again?

The Route Planners

Scheduling find the path. They plan the safest path the get us to where our customers need us to be.

The Heros

Real-time take action to keep the wheels rolling, to keep us in a straight line, to do what it takes so we don’t stop.

The Bar Stool

At the end of the day the team get together to share stories. What went well, what failed and how can we do it better next time. This my friends is Analysis.

So what do Workforce Planning do?

Put simply, as George Ezra once said. we ride “Shot Gun”. (You’re going to be humming this while you read, sorry) Riding shotgun was used to describe the guard who rode alongside a stagecoach driver, ready to use his shotgun to ward off bandits. In modern use, it refers to the practice of sitting alongside the driver in a moving vehicle. The phrase has been used to mean giving actual or figurative support or aid to someone in a situation.[1] The coining of this phrase dates to 1905 at latest. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riding_shotgun

As a Leader you are the stagecoach driver, you control the direction, you control the speed, you are in control of the where, the when, the how and the why.

However, your focus is and should be on the road ahead. What challenges, obstacles or difficulties are the horses facing? This is where Workforce Planning comes in riding shotgun. We are here to ward off bandits. Well not really, what we do do is. While you're watching the road ahead, we’re looking at what's coming down the track, on your left and on your right. Is there anything coming that will slow us down, knock us off course, or maybe we can see a faster path we can take? We look at what happened, did we manage to cross that last river well? Can we do it better next time, can we do it safer? Can we avoid getting our passengers wet? We have lovely knowledge we know if we are entering bandit country. We’ll point out possible dangers ahead, we’ll remove what obstacles we can and we’ll work with you to cross that damn river.
